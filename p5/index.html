About Us

NewJeans (Korean: 뉴진스; RR: Nyujinseu), also known as NJZ (엔제이지; Enjeiji), is a South Korean girl group formed by ADOR, a subsidiary of Hybe Corporation. The group consists of five members: Minji, Hanni, Danielle, Haerin, and Hyein. The group pre-released their debut single "Attention" on July 22, 2022, ahead of their debut extended play, which was released on August 1, 2022.

The name NewJeans has two meanings. It alludes to the idea that jeans are a timeless garment and the group's desire to carve out a timeless image for themselves. It is also a play on the phrase "new gen," and refers to the group ushering in a new generation of pop music. In February 2025, the members changed their name to "NJZ" amidst their contract dispute with Hybe. Following a court ruling regarding the dispute, the group discontinued the use of "NJZ" in April.


2019–2021: Formation and pre-debut activities

NewJeans is a girl group project that began preparations in early 2019 as a collaboration between Big Hit Entertainment and Source Music, led by Min Hee-jin, who had just joined as Chief Executive Officer. Global auditions were held from 2019 to early 2020. In late 2021, the project was transferred to the new label ADOR after Min Hee-jin became CEO. Further auditions were held until early 2022, and the final lineup was finalized in March 2022. The group, once dubbed "Min Hee-jin's Girl Group," was originally scheduled to debut in 2021 but was delayed due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Before their debut, some members were already active in the entertainment industry: Danielle appeared on the variety show Rainbow Kindergarten (2011), Hyein debuted in the children's group U.SSO Girl (2017) and later joined Play With Me Club until 2021, while Hanni and Minji appeared as cameos in BTS's "Permission to Dance" music video (2021).


2022–2023: Introduction, Debut and Get Up

NewJeans was officially launched in July 2022 through a surprise strategy, starting with the unpromoted release of the music video for "Attention," which immediately captured public attention. They then released the singles "Hype Boy" and "Hurt," followed by their debut mini-album, which surpassed one million physical sales and became the best-selling debut album by a female K-pop artist in South Korea. Despite controversy over the lyrics of "Cookie," the group still earned numerous rookie awards and debuted on various music programs. In late 2022, they achieved further success with "Ditto" and "OMG," which charted on the Billboard Hot 100 and solidified their international reputation. Throughout 2023, NewJeans further solidified their global status through major collaborations with Coca-Cola and the release of their second mini-album, *Get Up*, which sold over 1.6 million copies in its first week. The singles "Super Shy," "ETA," and "Cool With You" were highly successful, reaching number one on various Korean charts and performing strongly internationally. They have also performed on global festival stages like Lollapalooza and are scheduled to perform at Summer Sonic, making NewJeans one of the fastest-growing and most influential K-pop girl groups of their generation.


2024–present: Japanese debut, upcoming album and conflict with Hybe

Starting in April 2024, a major conflict arose between NewJeans, Min Hee-jin, and Hybe following Min's dismissal as ADOR CEO. NewJeans members publicly defended Min and revealed allegations of harassment, discrimination, content deletion, and personal data leaks by management. They issued an ultimatum to Hybe to reinstate Min, which was rejected. Tensions escalated when Hanni testified at the National Assembly alleging unfair treatment, and NewJeans sent ADOR a notice of contract termination. Despite winning several awards, their on-stage statements sparked rumors of disbandment. In November 2024, Min resigned from ADOR, while NewJeans stated they wanted to leave the agency and retain the group's name—a claim ADOR denied and sparked a legal dispute.

As 2025 entered, the conflict became more complex. Industry bodies like KMF criticized NewJeans' actions, while media outlets like Dispatch accused Min of orchestrating the conflict—allegations the group denied and countered with a defamation lawsuit. The five members then opened personal Instagram accounts, faced new legal restrictions from ADOR, and in February 2025 introduced the new group name NJZ. They performed at ComplexCon Hong Kong before being ordered to cease all independent activities. After releasing their new song, "Pit Stop," the group announced an indefinite hiatus while they continued to appeal the court ruling in favor of ADOR.
